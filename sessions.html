Sessions

Assessment. Feedback. Connection.

1

First meeting

  • Complete a series of forms about you, your child, and your family
  • Interview you about your child's developmental history, emotions, and behaviors
  • Discuss how you are feeling as a parent
  • Review your family's strengths and challenges

2

Second meeting

  • Observe you and your child interacting to help identify additional strengths and challenges
  • Review your thoughts about the interaction
  • Provide initial feedback and information
  • Begin discussing goals for your family

3

Third meeting

  • Provide written feedback and recommendations
  • Help you create an action plan to achieve your family goals and troubleshoot obstacles
  • Inform you about opportunities for low-cost services at our center or elsewhere

About Your Session:

At Family Check-In, we are committed to working with you to assess the individual needs of your family. We will meet with you and your child in our Psychological Services Center (PSC) at LIU Post.

Why is it Important?

Research has shown that early intervention with parents and children can prevent future emotional and behavioral problems. Family Check-In is a low-cost program that can provide parents with knowledge and skills to build upon their strengths and support changes that will help them reach their goals.
